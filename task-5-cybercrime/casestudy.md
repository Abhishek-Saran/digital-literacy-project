# Case Study: UPI/ Online Payment Fraud

## Introduction
* UPI fraud is a type of cybercrime in which the attacker manipulates the user to send money or provide sensitive information to them. This type of fraud has been a lot more with the emergence of digital payments in India.

## How the Fraud Happens
* A typical fraud starts with a call, message, or link from a person posing as a bank official, customer care executive, buyer, etc. The attacker tries to obtain the trust of the victim by asking them to approve a "request" or to scan a QR code. In many cases the victim is told that they will be sent money, but what actually is occurring is that the victim is authorizing a payment. Once the victim types their UPI PIN the money is instantly transferred to the scammers account.

## Targeted Victims
* Students, aged individuals, and those who are not familiar with digital payment systems are the most favored targets. Fraudsters exploit a sense of urgency, confusion, or lack of knowledge in order to complete the scam effectively.

## Consequences
* The consequences can be obvious. financial loss, stress and loss of trust in digital platforms. As UPI transactions are instant, it is often not easy to recover money.

## Conclusion
* This case proves the point of digital literacy and awareness. Users should never share their UPI PIN, unknown links should be avoided, and payment should also be carefully verified to be safe online.
